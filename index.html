Despite its existence for thousands of years, freediving has become relatively well-known and widespread only recently. In 1949, the Italian Raimondo Bucher[it] reached a depth of 30 meters in apnea. By 1953, Enio Falco and Alberto Novelli, competing with Bucher, crossed the 40-meter line. Bucher's passion for underwater film and photography also served to popularize underwater sports.

Spearfishing (underwater fishing) is a method of recreational fishing based on the extraction of aquatic biological resources (fish) by individual non-mass fishing tools - hands, harpoons, harpoon spear guns, including search, detection, pursuit (either stalking or luring) and fishing in conditions of free movement of the underwater hunter and the object of fishing in the aquatic environment.
